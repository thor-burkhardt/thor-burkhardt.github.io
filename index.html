Intro

Hi––I'm Thor! Originally from Northern Virginia, I am a rising junior studying math & physics at Brown University. Click through the next few pages to learn about who I am and what I do.

Above you can see one of my proudest (and most unexpected) moments: bearing the Rhode Island state flag at Brown's 2024 commencement.

computational physics

I have been in Brown's Quantum Computing Group since 2023, helping apply quantum algorithms to dynamical systems (such as the climate, biomolecules, and financial markets). Our group focuses on reducind these systems to Hamiltonian problems which can take advantage of (theoretically) efficient quantum eigensolvers. Most recently, I have been casting the Lorenz attractor (the famous, unsolved "butterfly effect") into a finite basis approximation of Hermite polynomials to form such a Hamiltonian (above).

nanotech

I have been working in the K-Lab on soft matter fabrication and measurement since Spring 2024. In particular, my research has centered on nanofluid osmotics in micron-scale channels. Over the course of summer 2024, I will have be experienced in nanofabrication of 2d materials, atomic force microscopy (AFM), and Raman spectroscopy.
